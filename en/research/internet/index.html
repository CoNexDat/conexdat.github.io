    Internet

    Internet tomography and topology modelling (BGP, IXPs, AS-level). We design routing protocols whose tables grow sub-linearly with network size.

    We study Internet structure from three complementary angles:

    • Tomography, both active and passive — topological exploration via traceroute-like probes, analysis of measurement bias, and discovery of hidden MPLS tunnels.
    • AS-level topology and BGP — modelling the autonomous-system graph, k-core decomposition of the Internet, and tracking the rise of content providers (CDNs) in the network core.
    • Internet Exchange Points (IXPs) in Latin America, with a special focus on developing countries where IXP deployment radically changes Quality of Service and traffic economics.

    We also propose EGP routing protocols whose tables grow sub-linearly with network size, and contribute at the IETF (RFC 9198 — AURA) on standardising unidirectional measurements.

    Featured collaboration

    Hernán Galperin (USC Annenberg, ex-Universidad de San Andrés)

    We collaborate with Dr. Hernán Galperin, researcher at USC Annenberg (formerly Universidad de San Andrés), on the Internet Society project that led to the first IXPs of Bolivia and Paraguay. The work combines active network measurement with an economic and regulatory analysis of how IXPs change Internet access in developing countries.
